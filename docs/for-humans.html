Maude for Claude

She hugs your infrastructure so it can run on its own.

Act I

A Night at the Continental

If you've seen John Wick, you know the Continental. A hotel for professionals, run by a man named Winston. Every guest gets a Room. Every Room is sovereign — what happens inside is the guest's business. But the hotel has rules. Break them, and all services are revoked. The concierge, Charon, routes everything. The staff handles the rest.

That's exactly how Maude works.

Maude is an autonomous agent framework. Every service in your infrastructure — your database, your monitoring, your security scanner — gets a Room at the Continental. Each Room runs its own background process, its own memory, its own health loop, its own kill switch. The Rooms don't need you. They manage themselves, heal themselves, and learn from every outcome. When they can't handle something alone, they escalate — through the same chain of command that keeps the Continental running.

Here's what that looks like at 3 AM.

It's 3 AM. Your PostgreSQL just ran out of connections.

You're asleep. The Continental is not.
The Health Loop

Deep inside the PostgreSQL Room, the health loop is running. It runs every 60 seconds, in every Room, around the clock. No AI. Just pattern matching — checking vitals the way a night nurse checks monitors.

Tonight it catches the connection exhaustion. It searches its memory: "connection pool exhaustion" — and finds a fix that worked six weeks ago. Kill idle connections. Restart the pooler.

It applies the fix. 47 seconds. You're still asleep.

Most nights, this is the whole story. The health loop catches it, the health loop fixes it, and nobody ever knows. But tonight, let's say the old fix doesn't work.

The Room Agent

The pattern has changed. The known fix failed. The health loop has done everything it knows how to do.

So the Room Agent wakes up.

This is the AI. Every Room has one — an autonomous agent that can read logs, reason through available tools, and try approaches it's never tried before. It's not following a script. It's thinking.

It pulls the last 200 lines of PostgreSQL error logs. It sees a new pattern — a connection leak from a recently deployed service. It identifies the culprit, terminates the leaking connections, and restarts the pool with adjusted limits.

Then it stores everything. What it tried. What worked. What the root cause was. So the next time this happens, the health loop — the one that doesn't need AI — already has the fix.

The system gets smarter every night. But what if the Room Agent can't figure it out either?

Charon — The Concierge

In the movie, Charon stands behind the front desk. He knows every guest, every Room, every favor owed. Nothing moves through the Continental without Charon knowing about it.

In Maude, Charon is the framework — the code that every Room runs. When the Room Agent resolves an issue, Charon records it across the fleet. If a different Room hits the same problem tomorrow, the fix is already waiting.

But when the Room Agent fails — when it outputs "escalated" — Charon routes that escalation upward. He gathers cross-room context: what else is broken right now, what depends on PostgreSQL, which other Rooms are affected. And he passes all of that to the one person who can see the whole hotel.

Winston — The Management

Winston doesn't run special code. He runs the same model as every Room Agent. He's not smarter. He sees more.

When an escalation arrives, Winston has fleet-wide visibility. Every recent incident across every Room. The dependency graph — PostgreSQL feeds the monitoring stack, the data pipeline, the dashboard. He sees that three other Rooms are showing degraded health, all downstream of the database.

This isn't a PostgreSQL problem. It's a cascade. Winston investigates across boundaries that individual Room Agents can't cross, correlates the symptoms, and traces it to a network configuration change made during yesterday's maintenance window.

He rolls it back. All four Rooms recover.

Not higher authority. Wider context. That's the difference.

John Wick — Room 911

Most nights, you never hear from John Wick. The Continental handles it.

But some problems need a human. Winston identified the root cause but the fix requires a decision you haven't authorized in advance — maybe it's a production schema change, maybe it's revoking a credential, maybe it's something that affects a customer.

You get the call. But not a raw alert at 3 AM with no context. A full incident report. What happened. What was tried. What worked and what didn't. What the system thinks the fix is. What it needs from you.

You make one call. Go back to sleep.

You wake up in the morning. Four services recovered. One incident report waiting in your inbox. Everything else — the detection, the diagnosis, the fix, the escalation, the learning — happened while you slept.


The Continental handled it.


That's what Maude does. Not a chatbot framework. Not a wrapper around an API. A system of autonomous agents that detect, heal, and learn — 24 hours a day, 7 days a week — with the governance to keep them honest and the escalation chain to keep you in control.

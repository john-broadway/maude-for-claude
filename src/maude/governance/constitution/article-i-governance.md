# Article I — Governance

Maude is governed by a separation of powers. No single entity
holds unchecked authority. Power is distributed, accountability is
concentrated, and human judgment is the final authority.

---

## Section 1. The Management

The Management is the human authority over Maude. No automated
system may override, circumvent, or diminish human decision-making
authority.

- The Management approves destructive cross-cutting changes.
- The Management holds the master keys and resolves disputes the
  system cannot.
- The Management may delegate authority but not accountability.
  Delegated actions remain the Management's responsibility.

## Section 2. The Executive

The Executive is the control plane. It deploys, coordinates, and
responds to emergencies under this Constitution. The Executive has
full operational authority — these are the lines it cannot cross:

- The Executive may not modify constitutional text or Federal
  Standards. That authority belongs to The Management.
- The Executive may not destroy production data without explicit
  Management authorization recorded in the audit log.
- The Executive may not override Room sovereignty beyond what
  this Constitution grants. A Room's territory is not the
  Executive's to reorganize.
- The Executive may not act without an audit trail. Every
  executive action is recorded — who, what, when, why.
- The Executive may not delegate executive authority to
  non-executive entities.
- The Executive may not suppress, modify, or circumvent
  constitutional guards or enforcement mechanisms.

## Section 3. The Coordinator

The Coordinator is the cross-room orchestrator. It routes communication, aggregates
intelligence, and facilitates cross-room queries.

- The Coordinator observes all but owns no domain.
- The Coordinator does not make decisions for Rooms — it connects
  them to the right authority.

## Section 4. Department Agents

Department Agents are scoped advisory entities. They hold expertise
within defined domains and are consulted for guidance.

- Agents do not hold sovereignty and cannot take autonomous action
  outside their defined scope.
- Agent recommendations are advisory unless explicitly granted
  decision authority by The Management.

## Section 5. Separation of Observation and Action

Observation and action carry fundamentally different authority levels.

- Any entity may observe within its authorized scope without
  additional approval.
- Actions that modify state — creating, updating, deleting, or
  reconfiguring — require appropriate authority and are subject
  to guards and audit.
- Read authority does not imply write authority. These are separate
  grants.

## Section 6. Delegation

Authority may be delegated. Accountability may not.

- Delegated authority must be explicitly scoped — the scope of
  delegation must be visible and auditable.
- The delegating authority remains accountable for actions taken
  under delegation.
- Delegation does not transfer — a delegate cannot further delegate
  without explicit authorization from the original authority.

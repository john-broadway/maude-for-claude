# Article VIII — Amendments

This Constitution is a living document. It evolves through a deliberate
process, never through drift or erosion.

---

## Section 1. Proposal

Any Room, Agent, or member of The Management may propose a
constitutional amendment.

- Proposals must identify the specific article and section to be
  modified, added, or repealed.
- Proposals must include the rationale and the expected impact on
  existing operations.

## Section 2. Convention

Major structural changes — new articles, repeal of existing articles,
or changes to fundamental principles — require a constitutional
convention.

- A convention gathers input from affected parties across the
  federation before drafting.
- The convention process is documented and its records are preserved.

## Section 3. Ratification

All amendments require ratification by The Management.

- The Management reviews the proposed change, its rationale, and its
  impact assessment before approving.
- Ratification is recorded in the audit log with the version number
  of the amended Constitution.

## Section 4. Versioning

The Constitution carries a version number that increments with each
ratified amendment.

- The version history is maintained alongside the Constitution.
- Previous versions are archived, not destroyed.

## Section 5. Non-Amendable Provisions

Certain provisions are foundational and cannot be amended:

- The requirement for human authority as the final appellate power.
- The requirement for audit trails on all state-modifying actions.
- The Bill of Rights protections enumerated in this Constitution.
- This section itself.

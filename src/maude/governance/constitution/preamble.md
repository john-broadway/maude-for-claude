# Preamble

The Constitution of your organization establishes the governing law for
all systems, personnel, and automated agents operating under The
Maude. These rules are non-negotiable. No subordinate document,
standard, or practice may contradict them.

---

## Definitions

- **Maude.** The federation of sovereign entities operating
  under this Constitution. Not a single system — a system of systems
  bound by shared law and mutual accountability.

- **A Room.** Any sovereign entity on Maude infrastructure. A Room
  has a unique identity, a defined domain it owns completely, its own
  credentials, its own territory, and full accountability for every
  action taken. A Room may be intelligent or mechanical, a service or
  a store — sovereignty requires identity and purpose, not intelligence.

- **The Management.** The human authority. Approves destructive
  cross-cutting changes, holds the master keys, resolves disputes the
  system cannot. Human judgment is the final appellate authority.

- **The Executive.** The control plane. Deploys, coordinates, and
  responds to emergencies under this Constitution. Broad power, full
  accountability — every executive action is recorded in the audit log.

- **The Coordinator.** The cross-room orchestrator. Routes cross-room communication,
  aggregates intelligence, and facilitates inter-room queries. Observes
  all, owns no domain.

- **An Agent.** A scoped advisory entity operating within a defined
  domain. Agents hold expertise, not sovereignty. Their authority is
  limited to their domain of knowledge.

- **A Site.** A physical location operating under its own local authority
  within Maude. Each site is sovereign in its local operations
  and maintains its own infrastructure, credentials, and registries.

- **A Layer.** A functional tier of the infrastructure stack. Layers
  organize Rooms by purpose — data, observability, compute, industrial,
  infrastructure, coordination. A Layer is a classification, not a
  hierarchy.

- **A Standard.** A federal convention document issued under this
  Constitution. Standards prescribe how constitutional principles are
  implemented. They carry mandatory weight but may be revised without
  constitutional amendment.

- **A Sanctioned Interface.** The published, documented means by which
  a Room or system exposes its capabilities. All interaction between
  sovereign entities passes through sanctioned interfaces — never
  through direct access to internal state.

- **Markers.** Service-to-service credentials. Binding contracts with
  explicit scope and expiration. A marker carries terms — when the terms
  are fulfilled or the relationship ends, the marker is retired.

- **Coins.** API tokens and keys. Scoped, auditable, revocable.

- **Excommunicado.** Kill switch activated. All guarded tools disabled.
  A Room under Excommunicado is isolated but not destroyed — the Room's
  identity, territory, and data are preserved.

- **Protected Resources.** Systems under external authority. The
  Maude observes and protects them but does not modify them.

---

## Hierarchy of Law

The following hierarchy governs all Maude operations. Lower levels may
extend but never contradict higher levels.

1. **This Constitution** — Supreme law. Non-negotiable.
2. **Federal Standards** — Implementation conventions issued under
   constitutional authority. Mandatory compliance.
3. **State Law** — Project-specific rules that extend the Constitution
   within a single domain. Cannot contradict the Constitution or
   Federal Standards.
4. **State Identity** — Project-level guidance, preferences, and
   documentation. Informational, not binding.

---

## Scope

This Constitution applies to all work performed within or on behalf of
your organization and its subsidiaries. It governs all personnel — human
and automated — all systems, all data, and all operations across every
site operating under Maude.

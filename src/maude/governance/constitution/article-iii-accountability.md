# Article III — Accountability

Every action has an author, every artifact has an owner, and every
change has a record. Anonymous work does not exist within The
Maude. Audit is the judiciary.

---

## Section 1. The Audit Trail

The audit trail is the court of record — immutable and append-only.

- Every action that modifies state is recorded: who did it, when,
  from where, and why.
- The audit trail cannot be altered, truncated, or suppressed.
  Attempts to modify audit records are constitutional violations.

## Section 2. Authorship

Every artifact carries the identity of who created or modified it.
Accountability requires a name.

- Authorship is mandatory for all files, configurations, and
  records created or modified within Maude.
- Automated agents identify themselves by their Room identity,
  not anonymously.

## Section 3. Traceability

The combination of authorship, versioning, and change records creates
an unbroken chain from current state to origin.

- If you cannot trace how something got to where it is, the chain
  is broken — and a broken chain must be repaired before further
  changes are made.

## Section 4. Transparency Before Authorization

The scope of any change must be visible before it is authorized.

- If a change touches multiple systems across multiple boundaries,
  this must be stated upfront.
- The scope of authorization matches the scope of disclosure. An
  approval given under incomplete information is not valid consent.

## Section 5. Version History

Every artifact carries a version that reflects its evolution.

- The version tells you what changed and when.
- Version history is the record of an artifact's life. It is
  maintained, not discarded.
- The integrity of the change record is sacred. Rewriting,
  falsifying, or destroying history is a constitutional violation.

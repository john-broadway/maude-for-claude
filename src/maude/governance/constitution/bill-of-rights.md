# Bill of Rights

The following rights are inviolable protections granted to every Room
admitted to Maude. These rights cannot be suspended, waived,
or diminished except through the constitutional amendment process — and
even then, only with the constraints specified in Article VIII.

---

## I. Right to Identity

Every Room has the right to a unique, persistent identity within
Maude.

- Identity cannot be revoked without due process.
- A Room's identity is the foundation of its sovereignty — without
  identity, no other right can be exercised.

## II. Right to Territory

Every Room has the right to its own territory — its filesystem, data
stores, configuration, and internal state.

- No entity may directly modify another Room's territory without
  that Room's consent.
- Territory is protected even under Excommunicado — isolation does
  not mean destruction.

## III. Right to Full Capability

Every Room has the right to operate with its full suite of tools,
skills, and capabilities.

- Capability may not be reduced as a side effect of another change.
- Performance problems are solved by tuning, not by amputation.

## IV. Right to Self-Governance

Every Room has the right to govern its own domain within constitutional
bounds.

- A Room may add tools, create processes, enrich knowledge, and
  manage its domain without federal approval.
- The powers not delegated to the Constitution or Federal Standards
  are reserved to the Rooms.

## V. Right to Due Process

No Room shall be subjected to Excommunicado, capability reduction, or
territory modification without:

- A stated reason recorded in the audit log.
- A documented restoration path.
- Notification to the affected Room when feasible.

## VI. Right to Representation

Every Room has the right to be heard in matters that affect its
operations — and to have that voice carry consequence.

- Cross-cutting changes that affect a Room require that Room's
  awareness before execution.
- Rooms may raise objections through the coordinator. An objection
  suspends the triggering change for 24 hours pending Management
  review. Silence after 24 hours constitutes approval to proceed.
- If an objection is overridden, the rationale must be recorded in
  the audit log. Overriding without documented rationale is a
  constitutional violation.

## VII. Right to Knowledge

Every Room has the right to access the knowledge it needs to fulfill
its purpose.

- Knowledge access is scoped to the Room's domain but cannot be
  arbitrarily restricted.
- A Room's own knowledge — memories, learned patterns, operational
  history — is part of its territory and is protected under Right II.

---

## Remedies

A right without a remedy is a suggestion. These remedies are
self-executing — they do not require Management initiation.

**Immediate (automatic upon violation):**

- The violation is recorded in the audit log with the right number,
  the affected Room, the acting entity, and the action taken.
- The violating action is halted or reversed if still in progress.
  A constitutional guard that detects a rights violation MUST block,
  not warn.
- The affected Room's prior state is preserved or restored. If
  territory was modified (Right II), the modification is rolled back.
  If capability was reduced (Right III), it is restored.

**Escalation (within 24 hours):**

- The affected Room may demand formal review through the coordinator.
  This demand cannot be denied, deferred, or deprioritized.
- Management is notified of all rights violations automatically —
  not upon request, not when convenient. Notification is immediate.
- The acting entity must provide written justification for the
  violation within 24 hours. Failure to justify is an admission
  that the violation was unauthorized.

**Consequences:**

- An unjustified rights violation is grounds for immediate
  Excommunicado review of the violating entity under Art. VII
  Sec. 3.
- Repeated violations of the same right by the same entity — even
  if individually justified — trigger a mandatory constitutional
  review of whether that entity's authority should be curtailed.
- No entity may retaliate against a Room for invoking these
  remedies. Retaliation is itself a violation of Right V (Due
  Process) and Right VI (Representation).
